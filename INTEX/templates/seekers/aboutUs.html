Background

The proposed Black Cyber Recruiter (BCR) website will be dedicated to promoting diversity and inclusion in America’s employment by connecting the Black community with employers who foster equal opportunity as part of their corporate policies.

Employers often try to find diverse pockets of qualified job candidates by recruiting with a focus on diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI). There are employer standard practices that put Black students at a disadvantage when trying to fill that specific target segment of recruiting goals.

Job inequality continues to plague Blacks in the US. In October 2020, the US unemployment rate for Whites was 6% compared to that of Blacks of 10.8%.

In a 2020 study by Handshake, an app that connects students on college campuses with open positions, it was found that:

    GPA cut-offs exclude a large percentage of Black students from being able to apply for jobs. Of students submitting applications, 57% were non-Black students and 40% were Black students. In fact, Handshake went on to say that "Last year we first reported that GPA isn’t an accurate predictor of performance and what attributes employers should look for instead."
    Rigid recruiting windows give little time for students to prepare and perfect their resumes and job applications. Handshake reported that "… Black students don’t have the same opportunities to iterate on their resumes, cover letters, transcripts, and letters of recommendation within the three week window when applications are typically open during the fall and spring” due to tending “to have more on their plates than their peers.”
    Nontransparent applications discourage Black students from applying. By clarifying compensation and reducing documentation beyond the resume, 13% more Blacks will submit job applications and if relocation assistance is provided, 36% more Blacks will apply.

The BCR website hopes to provide a platform to connect Blacks to companies that are interested in increasing Black diversity in their workforce by addressing the three problems stated above.
